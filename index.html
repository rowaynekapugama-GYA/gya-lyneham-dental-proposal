Part One

Executive
summary.

Two decades of strong clinical reputation. A team of four dentists. A loyal patient base built on word of mouth. And a digital presence that hasn't been seriously updated in three years — at exactly the moment when the practice in your own building is winning the Google search every patient runs before they pick up the phone.

Lyneham Dental Care has earned the right to be the dominant inner-north Canberra dental practice. The clinical foundation is there. The team is there. The location is there. And — significantly — the practice is one of the most technologically advanced dental clinics in the ACT, including being one of only two practices in the territory operating with a specialist surgical operating microscope. That clinical advantage isn't reflected anywhere in the practice's current digital presence. What's missing is the infrastructure to convert two decades of goodwill, and a genuinely differentiated clinical capability, into a steady, predictable flow of new patients — and to defend that flow against newer, more digitally aggressive competitors.

This proposal lays out a complete program to do exactly that:

  1. A website rebuild that replaces the current site (which last published a blog in April 2023) with a modern, fast, conversion-focused presence designed around the way patients actually search and book in 2026.
  2. A local SEO foundation that pushes Lyneham Dental Care into Google's local map pack for the high-intent searches that drive new patient bookings in the inner north — "dentist near me", "dentist Lyneham", "emergency dentist Canberra" — and the service-specific terms that drive higher-value treatments.
  3. A paid acquisition program (Google Ads + Meta) sized to the practice's capacity, designed to fill the books on the services where the unit economics make sense (Invisalign, cosmetic, new patient exams, emergency).
  4. A reputation and reactivation system that turns the practice's existing 20+ years of patient goodwill into Google reviews, referrals, and recall bookings — using the SmileOx CRM platform GYA specialises in for dental practices.
The single most important observation

Dental Embassy isn't your nearby competitor. They're in your building.

Dental Embassy operates from Suite 2, 45 Hall Street — the same address as Lyneham Dental Care. They hold a 5.0 average Google rating across 105+ reviews, a modern website, an active blog, two locations (Lyneham + Braddon), and they are aggressively winning the "Lyneham dentist" search. Every patient who Googles "dentist near me" within walking distance of 45 Hall Street is being shown both practices side by side — and right now, the patient-facing signals heavily favour Dental Embassy. Closing that gap is the single highest-value move this proposal makes possible.

The engagement has three tiers — Foundation (SEO foundation), Growth (SEO + Google Ads management), and Scale (the full lead-generation engine with SmileOx CRM integration, Meta lead-gen, social content production and reviews automation, all managed by GYA). The one-off website rebuild sits underneath all three. Pricing for each tier is in §10. Paid media spend (Google Ads, Meta) is always paid directly by the practice to the ad platforms — GYA never marks up media; the retainer covers strategy, build, and management only.

Strategic timing

The September refurb is the moment this entire program is building toward.

Lyneham Dental Care is doubling its capacity in September — from two chairs to four — as part of a full practice refurbishment. This changes everything about how the marketing program should be sized and timed. The 90-day build (June–August) becomes a coordinated pre-launch phase: the website rebuild, the local SEO foundation, and the patient reactivation campaign all wrap around a single strategic question — how do we make sure the new chairs aren't empty on day one? A dedicated refurb reactivation campaign sits at the heart of the plan, detailed in §07 Workstream 09.

What we
observed.

An honest audit of Lyneham Dental Care's current digital footprint. Not a sales pitch — a diagnostic. Some of what's here is already strong, and we'll say so. The rest is the opportunity surface this proposal addresses.

3.1The website (lynehamdentalcare.com.au)

What's working

  • The site exists, is indexed by Google, has a clear practice identity, and lists the four dentists with photos and credentials.
  • Service pages cover the core categories (general, children's, cosmetic).
  • Contact details and address are clearly visible.
  • The practice ranks for its own brand name (as it should).

What's holding it back

  • The content is dormant. The most recent blog post is dated 28 April 2023. Google's algorithm explicitly favours sites with fresh, regularly published content for local service businesses. Three years of silence is a signal to Google (and prospective patients) that the practice may have closed or stopped operating digitally.
  • The design feels dated. Patient expectations for what a dental website should look like have shifted significantly in the last 3–4 years. Newer competitors in the inner north present like modern hospitality brands; the current site reads as functional but uninspired.
  • No visible online booking. Patients in 2026 increasingly expect to book in two taps from their phone, late at night, without speaking to anyone. The current journey forces a phone call.
  • Limited conversion architecture. No prominent calls to action, no offer or new-patient incentive, no live chat, no exit-intent capture. Every visitor who doesn't pick up the phone is lost.
  • Mobile experience needs review. The majority of dental searches happen on mobile devices; mobile load speed and touch targets are an immediate trust signal.
  • SEO foundations are basic. Page titles, meta descriptions, schema markup, and internal linking can all be substantially improved without changing a single visible word on the page.

3.3Google Business Profile & the local map pack

The Google Business Profile is the most commercially valuable digital asset a dental practice owns. For "dentist near me" searches within Lyneham/Dickson/Braddon, Google shows three local map results above the organic listings. That three-pack is where 70%+ of patient clicks go.

Lyneham Dental Care has a GBP listing, but it appears to be under-optimised:

  • Reviews: Substantially fewer than Dental Embassy's 105+. The quantity and recency of reviews is the single biggest map-pack ranking factor.
  • Posts: No active "Google Posts" — a free weekly micro-content channel that signals practice activity to Google.
  • Photos: Limited recent photography of the practice interior, team, before/after work.
  • Q&A: Practice should pre-populate common patient questions; competitors are doing this.
  • Services menu: Detailed services menu with prices/from-prices is increasingly visible in local search and drives conversion.
  • Categories: Primary and secondary GBP categories should be audited and optimised.

3.4Reviews & reputation

The practice has positive reviews — patients praise specific dentists by name (Dr Patrick Huang, Dr Kennneth Ho, Dr Kaitlyn) and mention things like follow-up calls the day after a procedure and a welcoming reception. That kind of qualitative signal is gold and shows the underlying clinical and front-of-house experience is excellent.

The issue is volume and freshness. Dental Embassy is sitting on 105+ Google reviews from 5.0 average. The math here is brutal: when a patient compares two practices at the same address and one has 100+ recent reviews while the other has a handful, the choice is made before they look at anything else. There's no clinical reason for the gap — only a systems reason. This is solvable in 6–8 weeks with the right review-request automation.

3.5Social media presence

Limited active presence across Facebook and Instagram. For a dental practice with a 20-year history, this isn't a primary growth channel, but it is a trust/legitimacy signal — patients who Google the practice often click through to social to see "is this place still active?" Even a low-cadence presence (1 post per week, mix of team, behind-the-scenes, before/afters where consent allows, community involvement) earns its keep.

3.6Paid advertising

No detectable Google Ads presence on competitive searches; no detectable Meta advertising. This means there is a clean slate to build paid acquisition properly — and conversely, competitors are not currently bidding aggressively on the practice's potential keywords, which lowers the cost-per-click materially.

Part Six

The
opportunity.

Most of the work to be the dominant inner-north Canberra dental practice has already been done. The remaining work is digital, not clinical — and it's well within reach inside 90 days.

Four honest reasons Lyneham Dental Care can win this market:

6.1Twenty years of clinical credibility

Two decades in practice at the same address is a moat that newer competitors — including the practice in the same building — cannot replicate at any speed. The reviews that exist already point repeatedly to the gentleness, professionalism, and follow-through of named dentists. The clinical foundation is in place. The marketing infrastructure just hasn't caught up.

6.2One of the most technologically advanced practices in the ACT

This is the differentiator most patients don't realise exists. Lyneham Dental Care is one of only two dental practices in the entire ACT operating with a specialist surgical operating microscope — equipment that delivers a level of precision in endodontic, restorative, and fine cosmetic work that the vast majority of practices simply cannot match. Combined with the broader investment in advanced clinical technology that positions the practice as one of the most technologically advanced clinics in the ACT, this is a credibility signal that competitors — including Dental Embassy in the same building — cannot match. It is also the kind of clinical claim that performs exceptionally well in patient-facing content (website, paid ads, social, Google posts) once it's actually said. Right now, it isn't.

6.3A team of four dentists — and four chairs from September

Many local competitors are single-dentist owner-operated practices with hard capacity limits. Lyneham Dental Care can absorb significantly more new patient bookings without compromising chair time per patient — and that capacity is about to double. The September refurbishment expands the practice from two chairs to four, which means the marketing investment doesn't bottleneck against chair availability through the second half of 2026. A practice with four dentists and four chairs in inner-north Canberra is a meaningfully different commercial entity than the two-chair version of itself, and the marketing should be sized for what's coming, not what's there today.

6.4The catchment is one of Australia's best

High income, high private health insurance, dense walkable population, public-transit connected, professional demographic that values preventative and cosmetic dentistry. There are not many catchments in Australia where the patient willingness-to-pay supports cosmetic dentistry, Invisalign, and high-end restorative work at the volumes inner-north Canberra can.

The strategic positioning

Lyneham Dental Care is "the established practice, modernised — with the tech to back it up."

The brand story writes itself: 20 years in Lyneham. Four trusted dentists. One of the most technologically advanced clinics in the ACT, including specialist equipment found in only one other practice in the territory. The same address, the same clinical depth, the same patient relationships — now in a refurbished four-chair practice with the modern booking, the modern website, and the digital convenience patients expect in 2026. That's the positioning the new site needs to communicate, and the positioning every ad campaign should reinforce. It's a story Dental Embassy literally cannot tell.

Part Seven

The
plan.

Seven workstreams. Each addresses a specific gap or opportunity from the audit. Together they form a complete patient-acquisition system — designed to run continuously, with monthly reporting back to Faye and Dr Patrick.

Workstream 01 · Foundation
Website rebuild & conversion architecture

A complete replacement of lynehamdentalcare.com.au. Built on modern infrastructure, mobile-first, fast-loading, optimised for both search rankings and patient conversion. Full detail in §08. Single largest single-investment item in the plan — and the foundation everything else depends on. Without a new site, every dollar spent on ads and SEO funnels patients into a 2020-vintage experience that erodes conversion.

Workstream 02 · Always on
Local SEO & content program

The slow-but-compounding channel. We rebuild the SEO foundation (page titles, meta descriptions, schema markup, site speed, internal linking), publish suburb-specific landing pages for the inner-north catchment, restart the blog with 2 monthly articles on topics that match patient search behaviour, and execute backlink outreach to local Canberra publications and dental directories. Expected impact: meaningful map-pack visibility within 60 days, top-3 organic visibility on core keywords inside 6 months.

Workstream 03 · The free channel that matters most
Google Business Profile transformation

The single highest-ROI channel in local dental marketing — and currently underused. We rebuild the profile from the ground up: optimised primary & secondary categories, full services menu with from-prices where appropriate, comprehensive Q&A, weekly Google Posts (a 5-minute task that signals activity to Google), guidance to the practice on a quarterly photo refresh (phone-shot is fine — patients trust real over staged), and review reply templates for every new review good or bad. Goal: 50+ new Google reviews inside 90 days from existing patient base, taking the practice past Dental Embassy in raw review count.

Workstream 04 · Growth & Scale tiers
Google Ads — high-intent capture

Google Ads is the fastest lever in dental marketing. Patients searching "emergency dentist Canberra" at 11pm on a Tuesday want one thing: to book. GYA builds and manages dedicated Google Ads campaigns targeting the highest-intent search queries (emergency, near-me, specific service terms), routed to bespoke landing pages on the new site, with conversion tracking back through GA4 to attribute every booked appointment to the keyword that drove it.

How GYA charges for paid media: the retainer covers campaign strategy, build, management, optimisation, and reporting. Media spend (i.e. the budget that goes to Google) is always paid directly by the practice to Google — GYA never marks up media or runs it through our account. This keeps the engagement transparent and means the practice always owns the ad account, the data, and the audiences.

Workstream 05 · Scale tier only
Meta (Facebook + Instagram) — lead generation engine

This is the workstream that defines the Scale tier. Where Google Ads captures patients who are already searching for a dentist, Meta puts the practice in front of patients who aren't searching yet but should be. The play is Meta Lead Gen forms targeted at the inner-north geo (5km around Lyneham), specifically: new patient offers, Invisalign / smile makeover campaigns, kids' first dental visit, and seasonal moments (back-to-school, end-of-year private health benefits expiry). Every lead captured flows directly into SmileOx (also Scale tier) for instant SMS follow-up — typically within 60 seconds of form submission.

As with Google Ads, media spend is paid directly by the practice to Meta; the Scale retainer covers strategy, creative production, audience build, campaign management, and reporting. Budget is sized seasonally — heavier in spring/summer (cosmetic season) and December (private health benefits "use it or lose it" period). Right starting point agreed with Faye and Dr Patrick.

Workstream 06 · Scale tier only · Requires SmileOx
Reputation system — Google reviews automation

The current review gap vs Dental Embassy is the most fixable problem in this entire proposal — but the fix requires the SmileOx CRM infrastructure (Workstream 07) to be in place, which is why this workstream sits in the Scale tier. Mechanism: automated SMS request to every patient 30 minutes after appointment completion, with a one-tap link to leave a Google review. We A/B-test the timing (some practices win at "as the patient leaves the chair", others at "the next morning") and the copy. With a four-dentist practice seeing meaningful daily volume, the math is straightforward: 5–10 review requests per day at a 15% response rate = 25–50 new reviews per month. Closes the Dental Embassy gap inside 90 days.

Workstream 07 · Scale tier only
SmileOx CRM — the patient retention engine

The Scale tier centres on SmileOx — the dental-specific CRM platform GYA deploys across our 100+ practice network. SmileOx becomes the practice's automation backbone, covering: instant SMS follow-up to every Meta lead (typically within 60 seconds of form submission), new-patient welcome sequences, post-treatment follow-up, recall reminders (6-monthly check-up), reactivation campaigns for patients lapsed 12+ months, birthday messages, treatment plan follow-up, post-Invisalign-consult nurture, and patient newsletters.

SmileOx is normally $999 per month direct subscription. As part of the GYA Scale tier, this is offered at the discounted rate of $199 per month — included with the Scale retainer. The reactivation channel alone (emailing/SMSing the existing 20-year patient list) typically returns the cost of the entire CRM subscription multiple times over inside the first quarter.

Workstream 08 · Scale tier only
Social media — posting & content design

Also part of the Scale tier: GYA produces and posts to the practice's Facebook and Instagram on a regular cadence. This covers content design (custom graphics in the practice's brand), captions, hashtag strategy, and scheduled posting — mixing team content, before/afters where consent allows, patient education, seasonal moments, and Billable-Miles-style community engagement. The goal isn't to win on social as an acquisition channel (it rarely is for dental); it's to look alive and current to the patient who clicks through from Google to your Instagram before booking — the same patient who currently lands on a quiet feed and forms the wrong impression.

Workstream 09 · One-time campaign · Tied to September launch
Refurb reactivation & relaunch campaign

The single most important moment in the entire engagement. Doubling chair capacity in September is a one-time, high-leverage marketing event — and the program needs to deliver a calendar full of bookings into those new chairs from day one. This workstream is a dedicated, multi-channel campaign that runs in parallel with the ongoing program for roughly 16 weeks: 12 weeks of buildup (June–August) and 4 weeks of launch activity (September).

9.1 — Pre-launch buildup (June–August)

  • "Watch our practice transform" content series. Weekly progress updates from inside the refurb — construction photos, equipment delivery, team behind-the-scenes — published to Instagram, Facebook, Google Business Profile (as Posts), and a dedicated page on the new website. Generates anticipation and reactivates dormant followers.
  • Patient reactivation SMS + email campaign. Built on the SmileOx infrastructure (Scale tier). Three-touch sequence to lapsed patients (12+ months since last visit) framed around the refurb: "We've doubled our capacity. There's now room for you to come back." Pre-booking window opens 4 weeks before launch — early bookers get first pick of the new chairs.
  • Waitlist build. A "Reserve your appointment in our new chairs" form on the homepage hero from June onward. Captures email + phone, drops directly into SmileOx for nurture, populates a Day-1 launch booking queue.
  • Local PR & community outreach. Region Canberra, RiotACT, Lyneham/Dickson/Braddon community Facebook groups. The angle: local practice, 20 years at 45 Hall Street, doubling capacity to serve the inner-north community better. Free coverage in publications patients actually read.
  • Refurb landing page on the new website. Single dedicated page telling the refurb story, with the waitlist form, progress photos, FAQ ("Are you still open during the refurb?"), and the September opening date counter.

9.2 — Launch week & September acceleration
